All about this Gambling Awareness Course

Globally, it’s believed that 26% of the world’s population gamble, and this equates to 1.6 billion people. While this course will focus on UK statistics, it’s worth recognising how big the gambling industry is.

In addition, this course will also introduce the facts of gambling, such as who can gamble, while informing you of the different types of gambling. We’ll then explore the risk factors and signs, look at case studies, and explore the external influences that encourage a gambling culture.

This course will also provide you with information about the effects of gambling and help you get to grips with the reasons why a person might begin gambling and how it can spiral out of control.

We’ll also look at the negative side of gambling by reviewing the emotional effects and discussing how people who gamble can end up in debt, and even become so out of control, they feel compelled to steal.

Finally, this course will investigate how a gambler can be supported effectively by family and friends, as we explore the therapy available as well as blocking tools, links to organisations that can provide professional help, and the dos and don’ts when supporting others!

Units covered

  • Unit 1 – Introduction to Gambling Awareness – In the first unit of the gambling awareness course, you will explore what gambling is, the different types of gambling and UK statistics on gambling.
  • Unit 2 – Risk Factors and Signs of Gambling – In this unit you will explore the risk factors, signs and symptoms of gambling, as well as exploring gambling myths. You will also explore the external influences that can encourage gambling.
  • Unit 3 – Effects of Gambling – In this unit you will consider the reasons why people gamble and the effects it can have. You will also explore how gambling can cause mental health issues.
  • Unit 4 – How can Gamblers be Supported? – In the final unit of the gambling awareness course, you will explore how gamblers can be supported and how CBT therapy can support a person who gambles, as well as considering how gambling blocking tools can be beneficial.
